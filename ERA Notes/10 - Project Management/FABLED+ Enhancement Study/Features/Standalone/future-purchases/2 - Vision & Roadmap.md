# Future Purchases · Vision & Roadmap

> [FABLED+ root](<../../../_index.md>) · **Future Purchases** · [1 · Feature State](<1 - Feature State.md>) · [2 · Vision & Roadmap](<2 - Vision & Roadmap.md>) · [3 · Action Plan](<3 - Action Plan.md>) · [4 · Checklist](<4 - Checklist.md>)

## Enhancement thesis

Turn future purchases into an intentional decision pipeline that makes waiting, repairing, and declining as legible as buying.

## Business and household value

Intentional purchase decisions protect cash and reduce clutter while respecting joy. The moat is decision memory, not a larger wishlist.

The target is attention returned, errors prevented, decisions shortened, or conflict avoided—not engagement.

## Roadmap

1. Now — add a local decision snapshot and review date to one desired item.
2. Next — compare buy/repair/borrow/wait options with explicit evidence and partner positions.
3. Later — learn which desires persist and which outcomes produced satisfaction rather than regret.

## New opportunity set

### V1 — Desire half-life

- **Mechanism:** Track whether desire strengthens, stays, or fades at lightweight review points without gamifying restraint.
- **Smallest proof:** Review five items at 7 and 30 days.
- **Success measure:** At least one avoided or accelerated purchase feels clearly better informed.
- **Kill criterion:** Remove periodic prompts if they become guilt or noise.
- **Invariant:** Decay is descriptive; it never hides or deletes an item.

### V2 — Repair-or-replace dossier

- **Mechanism:** Compare remaining useful life, repair cost, replacement cost, downtime, and uncertainty.
- **Smallest proof:** Build a simple dossier for one appliance or device.
- **Success measure:** A real decision is made faster with fewer external notes.
- **Kill criterion:** Keep a free-text comparison if structured inputs are too burdensome.
- **Invariant:** Unknown estimates are labeled and never presented as facts.

### V3 — Shared purchase compact

- **Mechanism:** Capture each person's priority, contribution, privacy, and veto/consent state for genuinely joint buys.
- **Smallest proof:** Use a review card for one household purchase.
- **Success measure:** The final decision occurs without an off-app reconstruction of positions.
- **Kill criterion:** Do not use it for personal or low-cost items.
- **Invariant:** Silence is not consent and private gifts remain private.

## Existing-roadmap boundary

Future-purchase→transaction auto-complete and broad what-if simulation already exist; these ideas improve decision quality before purchase.

## Strategy guardrail

Start read-only or in shadow mode. Persist and notify only after real use passes the named gate; never create a parallel engine for an existing concept.
