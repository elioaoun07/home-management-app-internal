## New opportunity set

### V1 — Recovery budget

- **Mechanism:** Estimate interruption recovery and protect a small buffer around demanding work.
- **Smallest proof:** Show a read-only recovery warning on three busy days.
- **Success measure:** At least one unrealistic sequence is adjusted voluntarily.
- **Kill criterion:** Remove if it merely restates schedule pressure.
- **Invariant:** No automatic rescheduling.

### V2 — Minimum viable routine

- **Mechanism:** Offer a safe reduced version of a routine when capacity is low.
- **Smallest proof:** Define one routine with full and minimum forms.
- **Success measure:** The routine survives a bad day without becoming overdue debt.
- **Kill criterion:** Keep manual alternatives if switching creates friction.
- **Invariant:** Minimum form is user-authored, never model-invented.

### V3 — Attention recovery review

- **Mechanism:** Record only whether a focus block restored, drained, or had no effect.
- **Smallest proof:** Collect ten one-tap outcomes in an existing surface.
- **Success measure:** One scheduling preference becomes clearer.
- **Kill criterion:** Stop if check-ins are ignored.
- **Invariant:** No health or productivity diagnosis.

## Existing-roadmap boundary

Do not rebuild the deleted page, create another recurrence engine, or restate intent-aware day planning until the ownership gate passes.

## Strategy guardrail

Start read-only or in shadow mode. Persist, notify, or automate only after a real proof passes its gate.
